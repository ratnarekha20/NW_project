The Catastrophe of 80 Years Ago

80 years ago, the very first nuclear weapon was used upon humans. 6th August 1945, Hiroshima. Three days later, on 9th August 1945, the second was dropped on the city of Nagasaki.

It is estimated that 140,000 victims in Hiroshima, and 74,000 victims in Nagasaki have died by December of the year.

The bomb that was dropped released a glare as bright as the sun upon explosion. It is said that the heat at the center of the fireball exceeded a million degrees, heating the epicenter's surface temperature to 3000 to 4000 degrees Celsius within 3 seconds.

It is believed that the shockwave pressure was about 11 tons per square meter approximately 500 meters away from the epicenter. In Hiroshima, most buildings within a 2-kilometer radius of the epicenter were destroyed. Even those survived the heat and thermal radiation and the impact of explosion, many suffered the radiation poisoning. And they still do today.

Today, there are 106,825 survivors alive. The average age is 85.58. The number of witnesses to the calamity is dwindling.

In 1994, three years after the fall of the Soviet Union, Ukraine signed the Budapest Memorandum to give up the nuclear warheads, and in return, gain the security guaratee from Russia, the United States, and the United Kingdom.
